# Chapter 26: The Secret Procrastinator

The secret procrastinator is a master of illusion.

You go to the office. You sit at your desk. You open spreadsheets. You frown at the screen. You type loudly.
To the outside world, you are working hard.
But you know the truth. You are checking Reddit. You are planning your fantasy football team. You are staring into space.

You are "busy" all day, but you are doing nothing.

Then, at 5:00 PM, when everyone else goes home, the panic sets in. "I haven't done anything!"
So you stay late. Or you take the work home.
And now you are the "hard worker" who stays late. You are the "dedicated" one who answers emails at midnight.

But it's a lie. You aren't dedicated. You are inefficient. You are stealing time from your employer during the day, and stealing time from your family at night to pay it back.

The shame of the secret procrastinator is intense. You feel like a fraud. You live in terror of someone looking at your screen, or asking for a progress report on Wednesday when you haven't started.

You lie about your progress.
"How's that report coming?"
"Great! Just polishing the intro." (Translation: I haven't opened the file).

This deceit eats away at your soul. It erodes your self-respect. You know you are capable of better. You know you are lying to people who trust you.

And the worst part is, you are lying to yourself. You tell yourself you are "just researching" or "thinking."
You aren't. You are hiding.

It is time to stop hiding. It is time to come into the light.
It is time to do the work when you are supposed to do the work, so you can be free when you are supposed to be free.

Imagine the relief of saying, "It's done," and having it actually be done.
Imagine leaving the office at 5:00 PM with a clear conscience.
Imagine not having to lie.

That is what waits for you when you quit the rush.

### **Exercise: The Honesty Log**

**Objective:** To confront the extent of your "secret" procrastination.

**Instructions:**
1.  For one day, keep a log of what you *actually* do every hour.
2.  If you spent an hour on YouTube, write "YouTube." Do not write "Research."
3.  If you spent an hour staring at the wall, write "Staring."
4.  At the end of the day, look at the log.
5.  Calculate the ratio of "Fake Work" to "Real Work."
6.  Acknowledge the lie.
7.  Forgive yourself. The lying stops today.
