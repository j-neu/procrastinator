# The Easy Way to Stop Procrastinating (Passive / Overwhelm Edition) - Structure

**Target Word Count:** 15,000 - 20,000 words
**Tone:** Informal, authoritative, empathetic, direct. No references to Allen Carr.

## Table of Contents

1.  **The Most Disorganized Person I Have Yet to Meet**
    *   *Introduction to the author's own struggle with passive procrastination. The mess, the missed appointments, the constant feeling of "drowning" in simple tasks.*
2.  **The Easy Method**
    *   *Setting the stage: You don't need a PhD in productivity. You don't need a complex Notion setup. You just need to see the trap.*
3.  **Why is it Difficult to Start?**
    *   *Debunking the myth that you need to "get organized" before you can start. The catch-22 of "I'm too overwhelmed to plan, but I need a plan to stop being overwhelmed."*
4.  **The Sinister Trap**
    *   *How passive procrastination works. It's not a dramatic refusal to work; it's a slow drift into chaos. The belief that "later" you will have more clarity.*
5.  **Why Do We Keep Drifting?**
    *   *We know the mess is stressful. We know we're missing out. So why do we do it? (Relief from Decision Fatigue).*
6.  **The "Indecision" Addiction**
    *   *Explanation of the cycle. Putting off a decision gives a temporary hit of relief (safety). This relief is the "drug."*
7.  **Brainwashing and the Sleeping Partner**
    *   *Society tells us: "You're just messy," "You're scatterbrained," "You need to try harder." Debunking these myths.*
8.  **Relieving the "Overwhelm"**
    *   *The "pang" isn't physical pain; it's a foggy, heavy feeling of not knowing where to step. Procrastination doesn't clear the fog; it thickens it.*
9.  **Stress**
    *   *The belief that "going with the flow" is less stressful. Reality: Passive drifting creates a tsunami of stress.*
10. **Boredom**
    *   *The myth that we drift because tasks are boring. We drift even when we want to do exciting things but can't find the tickets.*
11. **Concentration**
    *   *How the "open loops" of undone tasks destroy your focus. You can't enjoy the movie because you're thinking about the unfiled tax return.*
12. **Relaxation**
    *   *You think you're relaxed when you're "chilling"? No, you're numb. True relaxation only comes when you are in control.*
13. **"Combination" Tasks**
    *   *Tasks that are both complex AND boring (e.g., sorting a year's worth of receipts). The ultimate trap.*
14. **What am I Giving Up?**
    *   *ABSOLUTELY NOTHING. The fear that you are losing your "freedom" to be spontaneous. You are gaining actual freedom.*
15. **Self-Imposed Slavery**
    *   *The weekend ruined by the background anxiety of the "to-do" pile. The feeling of being a passenger in your own life.*
16. **I’ll Save £x (or Hours) a Week**

17. **Health**
    *   *The stress of chaos. The sleep lost to 3 AM panic.*
18. **Energy**
    *   *Decision fatigue is exhausting. The energy surge of the decisive person.*
19. **It Gives Me "Space" (The Myth of Spontaneity)**

20. **Those Sinister Black Shadows**
