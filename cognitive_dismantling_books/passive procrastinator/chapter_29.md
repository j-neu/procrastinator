# Chapter 29: Will I Miss the Drama?

There is a dirty secret about passive procrastination: **We are addicted to the adrenaline.**

We drift for weeks, doing nothing. The deadline looms. It gets closer. The pressure builds.

Then, 24 hours before the deadline, we snap into "Hero Mode."
We drink four coffees. We blast music. We stay up all night. We type furiously. We feel alive! We feel like a wizard, pulling a rabbit out of a hat at the last second!

We submit the work at 11:59 PM. We collapse, exhausted but triumphant. "I did it again!" we think. "I work best under pressure!"

This is the **Drama Cycle**.

And we love it. It makes us feel important. It makes mundane tasks feel like life-or-death battles.

If we just did the work two weeks ago, like a boring person, there would be no battle. There would be no hero moment. There would just be... work.

So, will you miss the drama?

Yes. In the same way a person who quits gambling misses the heart-pounding terror of putting everything on Red.

But ask yourself: Is the drama actually *good*?

Look at the work you produced in that all-nighter. Is it your best work? Probably not. It's full of typos. It's shallow.

Look at your body the next day. You are wrecked. You are useless for 48 hours.

The "Hero Mode" is a lie. You are not a hero for putting out a fire that you started yourself.

You are an arsonist-firefighter.

When you stop drifting, life becomes much quieter. There are fewer 3 AM panic attacks. There are fewer frantic dashes to the airport.

Some people find this quietness unsettling at first. "Where is the rush?" they ask. "Where is the excitement?"

You have to find new sources of excitement. Healthy excitement.
*   The excitement of a new hobby.
*   The excitement of travel.
*   The excitement of deep, quality work.

The drama of procrastination is "cheap" drama. It is the drama of a soap opera.

**EXERCISE: The Arsonist Confession**

Pause.

Think of your last "Hero Moment." The last time you pulled off a miracle at the last minute.

Did you feel proud?

Now, re-run the tape. Imagine you simply did the work a week early.

You wouldn't have had the "high" of the last minute.
But you would have had a week of peace.

Would you trade the "high" for the peace?
If you say no, you are choosing stress as your entertainment.
Choose better entertainment.
