# CHAPTER 37
# SUBSTITUTES

When a smoker tries to quit, they often chew gum.
They are telling themselves, "I can't have a cigarette, so I'll have this instead."
This reinforces the feeling of deprivation. It says, "I need *something* in my mouth to survive."

The procrastinator has a favorite substitute: **Productive Procrastination.**
You need to write a difficult email.
Instead, you clean the kitchen. You organize your desktop icons. You research "productivity apps." You rewrite your To-Do list for the fifth time.

This is dangerous.
Because it feels like work.
You can tell yourself, "I'm not lazy! Look how clean my kitchen is!"
But you *are* avoiding. You are still in the trap.
You are using the cleaning as a pacifier to soothe the anxiety of the email.

**Do not use substitutes.**
When you have a task to do, there are only two valid states:
1.  Doing the task.
2.  Legitimately resting (not avoiding).

If you can't bring yourself to do the task yet, **do nothing.**
Sit in your chair. Stare at the wall.
Do not pick up your phone. Do not clean. Do not "organize."
Just sit with the task.

If you substitute, you are teaching your brain: "When the Main Task is scary, we can run away to the Safe Task."
You are keeping the fear alive.

Also, beware of **Planning as Procrastination**.
"I can't start yet, I need to research the best software."
"I need to color-code my calendar."
"I need to read one more book on the subject."
This is just sophisticated hiding. It is fear dressed up as preparation.

You don't need a substitute for procrastination.
You don't need to replace it with anything.
You are removing a cancer. You don't replace a cancer with a cold. You just get healthy.

***

### EXERCISE: The "Nothing" Challenge

**Objective:** To strip away the "Productive Procrastination" safety blanket.

1.  Select your Most Important Task (MIT).
2.  Set a timer for 10 minutes.
3.  Your rule: **You can do the task, or you can do nothing.**
4.  You cannot check your phone. You cannot clean. You cannot "plan."
5.  You will find that doing *nothing* is incredibly boring.
6.  The task will suddenly look very appealing by comparison.
7.  Start.
