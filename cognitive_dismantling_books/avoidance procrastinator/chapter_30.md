# WILL I BECOME A BORING WORKAHOLIC?

You worry that if you become "productive," you will lose your soul.
You will become one of "them"â€”the people in suits who love spreadsheets.
You worry you will become a workaholic.

This is a misunderstanding of what a workaholic is.
A workaholic is someone who uses work to avoid life.
A procrastinator is someone who uses avoidance to avoid life.
They are two sides of the same coin.

By stopping procrastination, you don't become a workaholic. You become **Efficient**.

The efficient person does the work, finishes it, and then *leaves*.
They go home. They paint. They dance. They play Dungeons & Dragons.

The procrastinator is the one who is obsessed with work.
You think about work 24/7. You worry about it in the shower. You worry about it on dates.
You are the one who can't let go.

Productivity is the off-switch.
When you finish the task, you get to turn the machine off.
You get to be a person again.

You won't lose your spark. You will finally have the time and energy to use it.

### EXERCISE: The Hobby Revival

**Objective:** To prove that efficiency fuels personality.

1.  Identify one "Soul" activity you used to love but "don't have time for" (e.g., drawing, reading, hiking).
2.  Finish your "Work" task one hour earlier than usual today (by focusing, not procrastinating).
3.  Use that hour **exclusively** for the Soul activity.
4.  Realize: The work didn't kill your soul. The procrastination did. Action revived it.
