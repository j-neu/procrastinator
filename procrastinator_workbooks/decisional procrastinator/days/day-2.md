## Why "More Information" Becomes a Decision Trap (2 minutes)

**Information Trap:** This happens when you delay making decisions because you believe you need more research about methods, more certainty about outcomes, or more data about "the best" approach. ## Main Exercise: Information Sufficiency Check (10 minutes)

### Step 1: The 70% Rule for Decision Making (3 minutes)

**The 70% Rule:** Research suggests that most good decisions can be made with 70% of the information you'd ideally want. Beyond this point, additional research often creates more confusion than clarity.

## Information Gathering Time Box (1 minute)

**Purpose:** Put boundaries around research to prevent endless analysis

**Time Box:** A predetermined limit on how long you'll spend gathering information before you must decide. This prevents research from becoming procrastination.
