## Understanding Task Complexity (3 minutes)

**Task Complexity** occurs when real life presents situations with multiple responsibilities, competing priorities, and mixed emotions. Mastery means navigating these situations while still taking effective action.

*(Real life rarely gives you simple, single tasks - usually you're managing several things at once)*

### Common Complex Task Situations:
*(Examples of mixed feelings and competing priorities)*
- **Excited and overwhelmed** about a major life change (moving, new job)
- **Motivated and exhausted** when managing ongoing family responsibilities
- **Proud and frustrated** with slow progress on important projects
- **Confident and uncertain** about major decisions
- **Eager and resistant** about tasks that involve other people
- **Grateful and stressed** about opportunities that require significant effort

### Complexity Navigation Principles:
*(Guidelines for handling multiple priorities and mixed feelings)*
**Both/And Thinking:** "I can feel motivated AND overwhelmed about this task"
*(Accept that contradictory feelings are normal)*
**Priority Layering:** Understanding which tasks are urgent vs. important vs. optional
*(Not everything needs the same level of attention)*
**Dynamic Management:** Recognizing how energy and emotions shift throughout complex situations
*(Your capacity and feelings will change - work with this, not against it)*
**Strategic Response:** Choosing which aspect of the situation to address first
*(You don't have to handle everything at once)*
**Integration Wisdom:** Finding ways to honor multiple needs while making progress
*(Look for ways to address several priorities through thoughtful action)*

### Step 2: Complex Task Navigation Practice (4 minutes)

**Practice the DANCE method for task complexity:**
*(DANCE is a framework for managing multiple priorities and mixed emotions)*

**D - Differentiate components (1 minute)**

*(Break down the complexity into specific, manageable pieces)*
