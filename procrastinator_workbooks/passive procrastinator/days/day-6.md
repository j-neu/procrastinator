## The Priority Overwhelm Problem (2 minutes)

When all your tasks feel important, nothing gets proper attention. Passive procrastinators often avoid starting projects because they can't decide which goal to tackle first, leading to a cycle of delay and stress.

**Today's solution:** A simple priority system that eliminates decision paralysis using the **Priority Matrix** - a tool that sorts tasks by urgency and importance to reveal what truly deserves your attention.

*Examples of priority confusion: Should I exercise, learn a new skill, organize my space, or call family? All seem important, so nothing gets done.*

## Priority Decision Rules

**To make prioritization automatic:**

**When overwhelmed by choices, I will:**
1. Use the priority matrix quickly (not perfectly) *(Example: spend 2 minutes sorting, not 20)*
2. Start with "Do First" quadrant only *(Example: ignore other quadrants until urgent/important tasks are done)*
3. Limit daily priorities to 3 maximum *(Example: choose 3 from "Do First," not 8)*
4. Remember: taking action on goals is better than perfect planning *(Example: imperfect progress beats perfect paralysis)*
